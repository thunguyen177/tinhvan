Duong blushed, "Why should we care about such an extreme event instead of the things that lie at the center region of normal distribution? For example, who are gonna clear the table and wash the dishes?" 🙄
"Well, extreme events are usually interesting, and have many applications," Quang, the postdoc researcher said, as serious as if he was giving a lecture.
"For example?" asked Duong.
"Like the bank can freeze a user's account when detecting irregular transactions, predicting storms, avalanches, tsunamis, epidemic, etc.," Quang said again.

"Well, you should minimize the loss, not to maximize it," Duong said,
"In the past, I trained neural networks, and the loss exploded many times. So, I guess similar things may happen when I try to pursue a girl." Quang said, with a lot of honesty 🙄
Everyone laughed again. 😂
"Then you use gradient clipping , coddle her, give her gifts when she seems indifferent to you." Duong couldn't hold her laughter. "By the way, it's not my turn to wash the dishes today. So, I'm gonna go back to my room and train my own network." Then, she left.
"I wonder, which loss function should we use for this problem?" Duy asked.
"Binary cross-entropy, of course!" Tuan smiled. "Suppose the output from a girl \(z\) is 1 if she likes you and 0 if she doesn't, and the probability that she likes you is \(p\). Then the binary cross-entropy is defined as $$z\log p + (1-z)\log (1-p),$$ which implies that if she likes you, then \(z=1\) and loss is \(log p\). If she doesn't, then \(z=0\) and loss is \(log (1-p)\).
That's for one girl. If you decide to pursuit \(n\) girls at the same time, then the loss is $$\sum_{i=1}^n (z_i\log p + (1-z_i)\log (1-p)),"$$
"Oh my god! Now I see who you really are. You pursuit \(n\) girls at the same time!" Quynh went ha ha ha ...
"Oh, no! For me, \(n=1\) for sure. Yet, I was afraid that he couldn't have a wife when he is too old. So, I thought that he may be interested in pursuing \(n\) girls at the same time."
"Who is older?" Duy rolled his eyes. 😤
"Of course, me!" Tuan replied. Then, he tried to move on to another problem immediately. "Let's talk about how to tackle this problem."
"Isn't this an imbalanced data problem?" Quynh grinned.
"Yes, of course, I mostly succeeded in pursuing girls. So this is really a very imbalanced data issue." Tuan quickly replied before Quynh could continue by saying that he failed to pursuit girls most of the time 😆.
Still, Quynh did't let go easily, "Is there any outlier, such as a widow or someone who is ten years older than you?"
"No"😤
"Maybe there is, but he already deleted it in his preprocessing step," Duy said. 😆
Everyone bursted into laughs again.
"This is a very small data problem. So, perhaps you should not drop a data point like that," Quynh said.
"Perhaps we should stop teasing him, before he DropConnect all of us!" Quang said, but his words itselves is another tease.
(Note: Dropout: At each training stage, individual nodes are either "dropped out" of the net with probability \(1-p\) or kept with probability \(p\), so that a reduced network is left; incoming and outgoing edges to a dropped-out node are also removed. Only the reduced network is trained on the data in that stage. The removed nodes are then reinserted into the network with their original weights. (Wikipedia)
In the training stages, the probability that a hidden node will be dropped is usually 0.5; for input nodes, this should be much lower, intuitively because information is directly lost when input nodes are ignored.
DropConnect DropConnect is the generalization of dropout in which each connection, rather than each output unit, can be dropped with probability \(1-p\). Each unit thus receives input from a random subset of units in the previous layer.
)
"So, are you gonna generate synthesis data using SMOTE or ADASYN, or Nearmiss to generate some synthetic girlfriends?" asked Quynh 😏.
  • SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique): rather than creating new ra ndom minority samples from existing minority classes, we over-sample the minorit y class by creating “synthetic” samples.
  • ADASYN: "The essential idea of ADASYN is to use a weighted distribution for different minority class examples according to their level of difficulty in learning, where more synthetic data is generated for minority class examples that are harder to learn compared to those minority examples that are easier to learn. As a result, the ADASYN approach improves learning with respect to the data distributions in two ways: (1) reducing the bias introduced by the class imbalance, and (2) adaptively shifting the classification decision boundary toward the difficult examples. (source: ADASYN: Adaptive Synthetic Sampling Approach for Imbalanced Learning
  • Nearmiss methods aim to under-sample the points from the majority class that is neccessary to distinguish between other classes. It's based on k-nearest neighbor clustering.

Tuan understood what Quynh really means by synthetic girlfriend. Yet, he ignored it and continued. "Those techniques are yesterday. I'll use customized loss function, "a specialist for imbalanced data" instead!"
"What is that?" Duy asked.
