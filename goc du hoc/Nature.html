Phytoplankton

Phytoplankton combine nutrients in seawater with energy from the sun. They also produce half the oxygen in the air we breathe. If the condition is good, they can extend so much that great swathes of the ocean turn green. Amazingly, they also play a crucial role in cloud formation. Moisture evaporating from the ocean condenses around tiny particales created by the plankton. These droplets combine to grow into colossal clouds. They can rise 20 kilometers into the atmosphere. These oceanic clouds reflect the sun's energy back into space, helping to protect the Earth from rising temperatures.
