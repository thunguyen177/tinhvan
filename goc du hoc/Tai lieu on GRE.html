Guides and resources for the verbal section Which word list should I learn first? My suggestion is that you should learn Magoosh’s word list first, then come Graduate hotline’s word list, Kaplan’s wordlist,Manhattan’s word list. Barron’s word list has too many words. Many of those words are not common in the test. Don’t waste time to study Barron’s word list.

Although most of the word lists is said by their creators “these are the … most important/basic/… word”, I don’t think that’s true! In most of the word lists, I can find “not really important” words. How to judge that? Just do practice tests, questions and read “The Atlantic, CNN, etc”.
In this page, we follow the above suggestion to create poems for you to learn English. When creating poems at this blogspot, I dismiss the words that I find unimportant or place them in the advanced word lists. I also give some notes on confusing words to help you learn better. Thus, don’t forget to study my commonly mistaken word list! You will find it very useful!
After learning basic wordlists, don’t forget to do practice questions. GRE verbal questions is all about choices and vocabularies.
If you have to prepare for not only the GRE test but also the  TOEFL/IELTS, then you should learn TOEFL/IELTS vocabularies before learning GRE vocabularies. The reason is  TOEFL/IELTS vocabularies are at the high school level, SAT vocabularies are at the university's level, GRE vocabularies are at the graduate school level.
