Recall that the Pearson correlation coefficient quantifies the degree of linear association between two sets of data. It falls within the range of -1 to +1, with 0 indicating no linear relationship. A correlation of -1 or +1 signifies a perfect linear relationship. Positive correlations indicate that when one variable () increases, the other () also tends to increase.
