Suppose that your little brother once saw half of a worm when someone ate apple. He feared eating apples after that. Now, you need to convince him that such a case can rarely happen at all by using a proportion test. More specifically, in this case, you can use a one-sample proportion test.

Null Hypothesis (): The ratio of worms in apples is equal to 1%

Alternative Hypothesis (): The ratio of worms in apples is greater than 1%

Let p be the proportion of worm in apples. Then, we can also write

Now, suppose you collect data from a sample of 200 apples, and you find that 3 of them have worms. You want to test the above hypothesis to see whether the worm infestation rate is less than 1%.

>

> # Perform one-sample proportion test

> test_result <- prop.test(worms_found, sample_size,

+ p = null_ratio, alternative = "greater")
