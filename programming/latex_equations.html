Multiline equation with amsmath library

Use 
\begin{eqnarray}
x &=&y\\
z &=&k
\end{eqnarray}
Note that for a better look, in the above equation, I used "&=&" instead of "&=" for this eqnarray environment

\small, \scalebox does not work while I'm trying to reduce the size of the equation?

Add the followings 
\setlength{\arraycolsep}{0.0em}
\setlength{\arraycolsep}{5pt}
above and below the equation, respectively. For example, change 
\begin{eqnarray}
a &=&b
c&=&d+e+f+g+h+i+k+j+l+m+g^2
\end{eqnarray}
into 
\setlength{\arraycolsep}{0.0em}
\begin{eqnarray}
a &=&b
c&=&d+e+f+g+h+i+k+j+l+m+g^2
\end{eqnarray}
\setlength{\arraycolsep}{5pt}
Note that the values 0.0em, 5pt are suitable for some 2 columns journal style.

'\left., \right.' does not work

You may have done sth like this 
\left(a+b &= c \right.\\
\left. & x^2=y \right)
or 
\left(a+b &= c \right.\\
& \left.  x^2=y \right)
Change the above to 
\left(a+b &= c \right.\\
 & x^2 \left. =y \right)
i.e., change '\left.' to somewhere away from the beginning of a new line.
