Some notes on research and writing papers

While doing research

-       Stay consistent to avoid extra works during revision: I did experiments for missing data and then when I plot the running time for because I think I would look nicer than 20%-40%. Yet, a reviewer asked why, and was there a specific reason? So, we added 50% experiments to the report.

-       The experiment result is terrible may be due to something wrong with the codes, not your method. So, check your codes before modifying your current method.

Writing and grammars

-       A good book on writing for computer science students is writing for computer science by Justin Zobel.

-       When trying to modify a table, some gigantic equations, remember to keep the older version by copying that part to some other file or comment it out in Latex (command 'Ctrl + /' in Overleaf).

-       Common sense should be used to check everything throughout. For example, in a missing data problem, the error rates should increase when the missing rate increase. Otherwise, the explanation should be made clear.

-       Say nice things before saying bad things. Tell the advantages of your methods/ how it surpasses other methods before revealing its disadvantages.

-       Consistent in writing order. For example, if you have two algorithms for two scenarios, which leads to two tables of results, one for each algorithm. The table orders and the corresponding discussion should be consistent with the order in which your algorithms are introduced.

Before you submit

-       Overleaf issue: Overleaf often displays the pdf file even if there are errors. Therefore, check before submitting to a journal

-       Not every journal/conference allows using extra packages. Avoid being rejected because of this!

-       References section: For big revenues, reference format is also important:
Make sure the following are available for each reference: journal/conference name, year of publication.
If the paper is published, cite the published version, not the archived one.
References should be in the same format (It's not good to see some have year-round brackets, others do not).
Bibtex copied from Google Scholar may not be perfect. So, make sure to check the consistency.

-       Check grammar throughout (even for short sentences)
