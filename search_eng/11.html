When NOT to "think outside of the box."

Regression! I love to talk to other people about that because it sounds full of regret. It's about trying to predict something based on the information you already have. For example, predicting tomorrow's stock price based on the stock's price in the past. Now, suppose that you are a farmer and have a cornfield. And you know from the past that the amount of corn you get depends on the amount of water. The data is as follows,

Water: 64 70 75 78 80 90 101 96 82 73 65 70 80 90 95

The question is if the amount of water is 200, will the amount of corn be 202? Let's think about this a bit before turning to the next page!


 

 

So, do you think if the amount of water is 200, the amount of corn will be 202? The answer is no. If the amount of water is 200, the amount of corn will be 0 because the plants would die before being able to produce corn!

So, what's with the rule

that we have mentioned before?

Nothing wrong with it! Just the number 202 falls out of the region that we examined the relationship between the corn and the amount of water. So, the rule above does not apply.  The fancy term for this in statistics is extrapolation. So, now you know that it's not always good to think outside of the box!
