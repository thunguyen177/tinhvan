The Baltimore parable

Once upon a time, in Baltimore, a man named X received a strange letter from a stockbroker. The letter revealed a stock that will increase sharply in the next week. A week has passed! As expected, the stock skyrocketed! The following week, X received a new letter from the broker. However, this time they predict that a certain stock will drop sharply next week. Indeed, it is! And so on, for ten weeks in a row, and every week the prediction in the letter came true. In the 11th week, you receive an offer to invest with the broker above, which comes with a request for a huge amount of brokerage money.

It may sound appealing. However, the reality is that the broker sent 1024 letters to different people to scam every week. That's the trick: Assuming the probability of correctly predicting the price of a stock up or down is 50%, this is like tossing a coin ten times to each person he sends a letter to. The probability of correct prediction for the first week is 1/2. The prediction of week 2 is independent of week 1, so the probability of correct prediction for week 2 is 1/2. The third week's prediction is independent of the first and second week, so the probability of correct prediction for the third week is 1/2...
